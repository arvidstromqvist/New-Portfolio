UX/UI Design

Routing Pilot

Improving usability in route planning system for electrical infrastructure

Role

UX/UI Designer

Timeline

2 months

Team

1 person

Tools

Figma

Industry

Energy

Year

2025/26

Goal

The goal of this project was to evaluate and improve the usability of RoutingPilot, an algorithm-driven power grid planning system.

The project aimed to produce concrete design solutions in Figma, grounded in user testing, cognitive science theories, and UX principles, in order to increase real-world value for engineers and planners

Problem statement

RoutingPilot is a demo of a powerful system for generating optimal routes for electrical infrastructure , but its interface had never been usability tested which may limit the system's real-world effectiveness

Unclear hierarchy

Projects, assignments and routes were difficult to distinguish and understand

Confusing Navigation

The sidebar made it hard to know where users were in the system

Route analysis

Route analysis lacked explanations, structure and visual hierarchy

Solution

A clearer, more intuitive routing experience

A usability-driven redesign grounded in user testing, heuristics and cognitive science

Improved hierarchy

"Assignments" were renamed to "Route packages" and breadcrumbs were implemented to clarify structure

Structured navigation

A standardized collapsible sidebar shows projects, route packages and routes clearly

Readable results

Route analysis is translated, grouped and enhanced with tooltips

Simpler templates

Layer weighting replace with intuitive prioritization levels and plain language

Design Process

A structured, research-driven approach grounded in user insights and cognitive science

01 Research

Understanding both the system and the domain of electrical infrastructure planning

  • Deep dive into RoutingPilot's algorithmic foundations
  • Research into energy planning workflows and comparable tools
  • Study of cognitive science theories and UX laws
  • Stakeholder meetings with delevopers at Iteract

02 User Testing

Identifying usability issues through 3 user-tests

  • Task-based tests with think-aloud method and semi-structured post-test interview
  • Participants were recruted based on degree
  • Qualitative and quantitative results

03 Heuristic Analysis

Evaluating the system with heuristic analysis

  • Using Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics
  • Gathering painpoints that was not noticed with only user-tests
  • Analysis landed on 19 pages

04 High-Fidelity Prototype

Final interactive prototype in Figma

  • An extremely interactive prototype in Figma
  • 30 main frames and 172 main variables
  • Grounded in user insights, heuristics and cognitive science
